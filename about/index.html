ABOUT ME

Cheng Kang studied his Master’s degree in Shenzhen University, China from 2013 to 2016. After graduated, he was served as a research assistant from 2016 to 2017 in the University of Hong Kong for the recovery research of spinal cord injury through deep learning methods. And he was also recruited as an algorithm engineer of AI from 2017 to 2018 at Wuhan Zoncare Ltd. to help cardiologists to diagnose heart diseases by 12-lead electrocardiograms in China. From 2018 to 2019, he was studying medical AI at the University of Leicester, UK. From the September of 2020, he was a Ph.D. student in the Department of Cybernetics, Czech Technical University, Czech Republic. He now mainly focuses on the area of Translational and Computational Neuroscience, trying to bring basic findings in neuroscience into daily life applications. For example, optimization algorithm with fuzzy logics in deep learning areas, selective and sustained attentions in Transformer blocks, medical chatbots for diagnosis, treatment sugession and recovery tracking.

I. Wearable BCI Systems: Mental Diseases and Movement Disorders

A. Bipolar and Unipolar Disorders >>>
1. Introduction

Mood disorders, includingmajor depressive disorder (MDD) and bipolar disorder (BD), are among the most debilitating diseases worldwide and with a lifetime prevalence estimated about 20.8% 1. Guidelines from the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, fourth edition (DSM-IV) , characterize the manic/hypomanic episodes as an index for distinguishing bipolar fromunipolar disorders, butmisdiagnosis of bipolar as unipolar could occur particularly when the BD patients come to the hospital in depressive episodes.

2. Our Contributions

Depressive patients present the brain hyperactivity of Beta band in some specific areas under working memory tasks2 , and these findings can contribbute the classifying and scoring of depressive symptoms with EEGs 3. Bipolar disorders show the theta differnece in some brain areas under facial emotional Stoop EEG tasks, and these can make the classification accuracy increase 4.

II. Medical Chatbot: Treatment Suggestion, Diseases Diagnosis and Follow-up of Recovery

A. Treatment Suggestion >>>
1. Introduction

The ultimate objective of the work is to create a neural agent capable of effectively delivering cognitive behavioural therapy. Although the primary target domain is smoking cessation, a large portion of the work is transferable to other domains where cognitive behavioural therapy is conventionally used today. The introduction first describes cognitive behavioural therapy and its principles, then presents viability, advantages and challenges of delivering it electronically. Smoking cessation specifics and relevant known facts follow. A short introduction to natural language processing and its potential use in cognitive behavioural therapy delivery is given with a more detailed summary given in the following chapters. Lastly, a succinct description of the set goals is given. Cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) is a set of therapeutical approaches aimed at fixing practical issues in a client’s life by identifying and overcoming cognitive distortions, replacing maladaptive patterns of thought with healthy alternatives, preparing coping techniques for physiological and emotional reactions to external stimuli and similar. A given therapy usually focuses on one specific problem the client is facing, like treating posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD), depression, bipolar disorder, or aiding in smoking cessation.
