1. Introduction

Mood disorders, includingmajor depressive disorder (MDD) and bipolar disorder (BD), are among the most debilitating diseases worldwide and with a lifetime prevalence estimated about 20.8% 1. Guidelines from the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, fourth edition (DSM-IV) , characterize the manic/hypomanic episodes as an index for distinguishing bipolar fromunipolar disorders, butmisdiagnosis of bipolar as unipolar could occur particularly when the BD patients come to the hospital in depressive episodes.

2. Our Contributions

Bipolar disorders show the theta differnece in some brain areas under facial emotional Stoop EEG tasks, and these can make the classification accuracy increase 4.

2. The Semantic Trap When Scoring Images or Symptoms

A. Depressive Severity Detection

What is semantic scoring trap? For exaple, for detecting depression severities, as categories of depression severities are severe={24, 25, … , 34}; moderate={17, 18, …, 23}; mild={8, 9, …, 16}; and non-depressed={0, 1, …, 7}, which have many similar features, and finally result in the high difficulty to classify or score them. If there is one male patient who is suffering mild depression, and his SCID-CD score is 16, but actually, many phschologists tend to diagnose his real symptoms as not dangerous. One typical way is to label the categories with scores, not only with the semantic description. Another solution is to design logic to infer the score that can represent the severity in detail when we could not get the severity scores.

B. Breast Cancer Detection

Another example is diagnosing BI-RADS in Curated Breast Imaging Subset of Digital Database of Screening Mammography (CBIS-DDSM) dataset. Overall BI-RADS assessment from 0 to 5 has been described in this dataset, including BI-RADS score 0 (Incomplete cases), BI-RADS score 1 (Negative cases), BI-RADS score 2 (Benign cases), BI-RADS score 3 (Probably Benign cases), BI-RADS score 4 (Suspicious Abnormal cases) and BI-RADS score 5 (Highly Suspicious Malignant cases). If there is a sub-score (another score from 0 to 9 which can measure BI-RADS score 3 in detail) that can provide patients with comprehensive information, the diagnosis and treatment would become more precise and convenient.

3. Medical Chatbot - Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, Diseases Diagnosis and Follow-up of Recovery

A. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

1. Introduction

The ultimate objective of the work is to create a neural agent capable of effectively delivering cognitive behavioural therapy. Although the primary target domain is smoking cessation, a large portion of the work is transferable to other domains where cognitive behavioural therapy is conventionally used today. The introduction first describes cognitive behavioural therapy and its principles, then presents the viability, advantages and challenges of delivering it electronically. Smoking cessation specifics and relevant known facts follow. A short introduction to natural language processing and its potential use in cognitive behavioural therapy delivery is given with a more detailed summary given in the following chapters. Lastly, a succinct description of the set goals is given. Cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) is a set of therapeutical approaches aimed at fixing practical issues in a client’s life by identifying and overcoming cognitive distortions, replacing maladaptive patterns of thought with healthy alternatives, and preparing coping techniques for physiological and emotional reactions to external stimuli and similar. A given therapy usually focuses on one specific problem the client is facing, like treating posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD), depression, bipolar disorder, or aiding in smoking cessation.

B. Diseases Diagnosis

1. Introduction

For example, there are 12 disease types: Esophagitis, Enteritis, Asthma, Coronary heart disease, Pneumonia, Rhinitis, Thyroiditis, Traumatic brain injury, Dermatitis, External otitis, Conjunctivitis, Mastitis. And 118 symptom types including explicit symptoms and implicit symptoms to protect privacy as much as possible. After using random forest with 50  max_depth to train this data, and the accuracy rate can reach 82.5%. Even trough the diagnosis rate can reach high enough, the real challenge is how accurate the chatbot can extract the symptoms from the dialogues!

Dialogue system for medical diagnosis challenge from Tencent Javis Lab.
