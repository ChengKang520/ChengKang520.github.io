ABOUT ME

Cheng Kang received the B.S. degree from Weifang University of Science and Technology, China in 2013, and the Master degree from Shenzhen University, China in 2016, respectively. He is currently a PhD student, researcher of RCI (Research Center for Informatics) for AI, and researcher of AID Lab, FEL, CTU. He is also recruited as a research assistant in Li Ka Shing Faculty of Medicine the University of Hong Kong at Hong kong in 2017 and a visiting scholar of University of Leicester in 2019. From the September of 2020, he was a Ph.D. student in the Department of Cybernetics, Czech Technical University, Czech Republic. He now mainly focuses on the area of Translational and Computational Neuroscience, trying to bring basic findings in neuroscience into daily life applications. For example, optimization algorithm with fuzzy logics in deep learning areas, selective and sustained attentions in Transformer blocks, medical chatbots for diagnosis, treatment sugession and recovery tracking.

Introduction Mood disorders, includingmajor depressive disorder (MDD) and bipolar disorder (BD), are among the most debilitating diseases worldwide and with a lifetime prevalence estimated about 20.8% [1](#refer-anchor-1). Guidelines from the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, fourth edition (DSM-IV) , characterize the manic/hypomanic episodes as an index for distinguishing bipolar fromunipolar disorders, butmisdiagnosis of bipolar as unipolar could occur particularly when the BD patients come to the hospital in depressive episodes. ##### 2. Our Contributions Depressive patients present the brain hyperactivity of Beta band in some specific areas under working memory tasks[2](#refer-anchor-2) , and these findings can contribbute the classifying and scoring of depressive symptoms with EEGs [3](#refer-anchor-3). Bipolar disorders show the theta differnece in some brain areas under facial emotional Stoop EEG tasks, and these can make the classification accuracy increase [4](#refer-anchor-4). Reference:

II. Medical Chatbot: Treatment Suggestion, Diseases Diagnosis and Follow-up of Recovery

A. Treatment Suggestion >>> ##### 1. Introduction The ultimate objective of the work is to create a neural agent capable of effectively delivering cognitive behavioural therapy. Although the primary target domain is smoking cessation, a large portion of the work is transferable to other domains where cognitive behavioural therapy is conventionally used today. The introduction first describes cognitive behavioural therapy and its principles, then presents viability, advantages and challenges of delivering it electronically. Smoking cessation specifics and relevant known facts follow. A short introduction to natural language processing and its potential use in cognitive behavioural therapy delivery is given with a more detailed summary given in the following chapters. Lastly, a succinct description of the set goals is given. Cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) is a set of therapeutical approaches aimed at fixing practical issues in a client’s life by identifying and overcoming cognitive distortions, replacing maladaptive patterns of thought with healthy alternatives, preparing coping techniques for physiological and emotional reactions to external stimuli and similar. A given therapy usually focuses on one specific problem the client is facing, like treating posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD), depression, bipolar disorder, or aiding in smoking cessation. ##### 2. Our Applications We developed a Mobel App - [ADIQUIT](https://www.adiquit.cz/en/), and you can view and download it.

B. Diseases Diagnosis >>>

##### 1. Introduction For example, there are 12 disease types: Esophagitis, Enteritis, Asthma, Coronary heart disease, Pneumonia, Rhinitis, Thyroiditis, Traumatic brain injury, Dermatitis, External otitis, Conjunctivitis, Mastitis. And 118 symptom types including explicit symptoms and implicit symptoms to protect privacy as much as possible. After using random forest with 50 max_depth to train this data, and the accuracy rate can reach 82.5%. Even trough the diagnosis rate can reach high enough, the real challenge is how accurate the chatbot can extract the symptoms from the dialogues! > Dialogue system for medical diagnosis challenge from [Tencent Javis Lab](https://mlpcp21.github.io/pages/challenge.html).
